### 1. Skill: Working Memory (The "Mental Sketchpad")

**Why it matters:** Working memory allows you to hold and manipulate information in your mind over short periods. It is essential for complex tasks like problem-solving, reading comprehension, and staying focused in distracting environments.

### 2. Exercise: The Reverse Span

*   **Setup:** Find a countdown timer or stopwatch.
*   **Step 1:** Set the timer for **2 minutes**.
*   **Step 2:** Find a paragraph of text from a book or article nearby.
*   **Step 3:** Read the sentences **out loud**, but reverse the word order of each sentence completely (e.g., *"The cat sat on the mat"* becomes *"Mat the on sat cat The"*).
*   **Constraint:** You cannot write the words down. You must hold the sentence in your head, reorder it, and speak it before moving to the next.

### 3. Reflection

*   **Question:** Did you find yourself "losing" the beginning of the sentence while trying to process the end? If so, did repeating the sentence faster or slower help you maintain the loop?
