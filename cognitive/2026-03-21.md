## Skill: Working Memory
**Why it matters:** Working memory is your mental "scratchpad." It allows you to hold and manipulate information in your mind over short periods. Strengthening this improves focus, problem-solving, and comprehension.

## Exercise: The Running Reverse
You will perform a series of simple arithmetic operations mentally without writing anything down.

1. **Step 1:** Pick a random 3-digit number (e.g., 482).
2. **Step 2:** You must add 7 to it, then subtract 3, then add 9.
3. **Step 3:** Take your final result and reverse the digits (e.g., 105 becomes 501).

**Do this entire calculation in your head.** Do not use paper or a calculator. Repeat the process 3 times starting with a new number each time.

## Reflection
**Question:** How much mental effort did it take to hold the initial number while performing the math steps, and did you feel the "slip" of information at any point?
