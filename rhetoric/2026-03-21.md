# Rhetoric — March 21, 2026

# Device of the Day: Anaphora

**Definition:** The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences to create rhythm and build emotional intensity.

***

### 1. Public Speaking
> "We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields and in the streets..." — Winston Churchill

### 2. Writing
> "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times, it was the age of wisdom, it was the age of foolishness..." — Charles Dickens, *A Tale of Two Cities*

### 3. Everyday Conversation
> "Look, I don't care if it's raining, I don't care if we're tired, and I don't care if it’s a long drive. We are going."

***

### Your Challenge
Write a three-sentence "Manifesto" about a minor inconvenience (e.g., slow Wi-Fi, a Monday morning, or a lukewarm coffee).

**Constraint:** You must start each sentence with the same three words.

**Example:**
* "**This isn't right.** The coffee is cold. **This isn't right.** The machine is broken. **This isn't right.** I am tired."
