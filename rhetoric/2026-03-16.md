# Rhetoric — March 16, 2026

## **Device of the Day:** Metaphor
**Definition:** A figure of speech that asserts two unlike things are identical for rhetorical effect, without using "like" or "as."

***

### **Examples**
*   **Public Speaking:** *"We are not just a political party; we are a family."* (Creates unity through implied kinship.)
*   **Writing:** *"The city was a concrete jungle, wild and unforgiving."* (Evokes strong imagery of chaos and danger.)
*   **Conversation:** *"I’m drowning in emails right now."* (Communicates being overwhelmed more vividly than saying "I’m busy.")

***

### **Your Challenge: The "Life is a Game" Rewrite**
Choose a current frustration or complex situation in your life. Describe it using a specific metaphor (e.g., "My job is a treadmill," or "This argument is a chess match").

Aim for one sentence that changes how you view the problem.
