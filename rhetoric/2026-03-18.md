# Daily Rhetoric Lesson

### 1. Device of the Day: Anadiplosis
The repetition of the last word of one clause or sentence as the first word of the next.

### 2. Three Examples
*   **Public Speaking:** "Fear leads to anger. Anger leads to hate. Hate leads to suffering." — Yoda
*   **Writing:** "They speak vain things. Things that are empty are nothing." — James 2:20 (variation)
*   **Everyday Conversation:** "I'm worried because if I lose this job, I lose my income, and if I lose my income, I lose my apartment."

### 3. Your Challenge: The Chain Reaction
Write three sentences explaining a cause-and-effect relationship regarding a current habit of yours (e.g., drinking coffee, checking your phone, exercising).

**Constraint:** The last word of sentence 1 must be the first word of sentence 2, and the last word of sentence 2 must be the first word of sentence 3.

**Example:**
1. I drink coffee to wake up.
2. Up is the only direction I want my energy to go.
3. Go get the mug.
