# Device of the Day: Chiasmus

**Definition:** A rhetorical device in which the order of words in the first of two parallel clauses is reversed in the second (ABBA structure) to create a memorable contrast.

***

### 3 Examples

1.  **Public Speaking:** "You can take the boy out of the country, but you can't take the country out of the boy."
2.  **Writing:** *"Ask not what your country can do for you; ask what you can do for your country."* — John F. Kennedy
3.  **Everyday Conversation:** "I work to live, not live to work."

***

### Your Challenge: The Flip
Identify a cause-and-effect relationship in your life (e.g., "I eat to satisfy hunger") and flip the structure on its head to create a chiasmus.
