# Daily Rhetoric Lesson

## 1. Device of the Day: **Chiasmus**
A reversal of grammatical structures in successive phrases to create an ABBA pattern (e.g., "You should eat to live, not live to eat").

## 2. Examples
*   **Public Speaking:** "Ask not what your country can do for you; ask what you can do for your country." — John F. Kennedy
*   **Writing:** "Hard work is often the easy work you did not do at the proper time." — Ibid.
*   **Everyday Conversation:** "I don't write to get paid; I get paid to write."

## 3. Your Challenge
Draft a "Before & After" statement about a personal habit. Flip the subject and object in the second half to reveal a deeper truth. Share it with a colleague or friend today.
