# Rhetoric — March 26, 2026

## Device of the Day: Anaphora

**Definition:** The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences to build rhythm and emphasis.

### 3 Examples

*   **Public Speaking:** "We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields and in the streets..." (Winston Churchill)
*   **Writing:** "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times, it was the age of wisdom, it was the age of foolishness..." (Charles Dickens, *A Tale of Two Cities*)
*   **Everyday Conversation:** "Look, I don't care what he said, I don't care what she said, and I definitely don't care what you think they meant."

### Your Challenge
During a meeting or conversation today, use **Rule of Three** (anaphora with three items). Start three consecutive sentences with the same phrase to drive your point home.

> *Example: "We need to save time. We need to save money. We need to save energy."*
