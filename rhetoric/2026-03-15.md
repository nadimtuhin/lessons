# Daily Rhetoric Lesson

## 1. Device of the Day
**Anadiplosis** (an-uh-di-PLO-sis)
*Repetition of the last word of one clause or sentence at the beginning of the next.*

## 2. Three Examples
*   **Public Speaking:** "Fear leads to anger. Anger leads to hate. Hate leads to suffering." — *Yoda*
*   **Writing:** "He possessed a cold mind; a mind that could calculate the trajectory of a bullet but not the trajectory of a soul."
*   **Everyday Conversation:** "We need to focus on efficiency. Efficiency will save us time. Time is money."

## 3. Your Challenge
**The "Chain" Exercise**
Write three sentences explaining a goal you have or a problem you are facing. Ensure the last word of the first sentence becomes the subject of the second, and the last word of the second becomes the subject of the third.

*(Example: I want strength. Strength requires discipline. Discipline is built daily.)*
