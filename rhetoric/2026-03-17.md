# Daily Rhetoric Lesson

### 1. Device of the Day
**Anaphora**
The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences to create rhythm and emphasis.

### 2. Examples
*   **Public Speaking:** Winston Churchill’s declaration, *"We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields and in the streets..."*
*   **Writing:** Charles Dickens’ opening to *A Tale of Two Cities*: *"It was the best of times, it was the worst of times, it was the age of wisdom, it was the age of foolishness..."*
*   **Everyday Conversation:** *"I don’t have the time, I don’t have the money, and I certainly don’t have the energy."*

### 3. Your Challenge
Write a three-sentence email or text message persuading a friend or colleague to do something simple (e.g., grab lunch, approve a project, go for a walk).

**Constraint:** You must start all three sentences with the exact same three words to drive your point home.
